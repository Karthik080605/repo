History

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre in Sydney, Australia. It is one of the 20th century's most famous and distinctive buildings. Designed by Danish architect Jørn Utzon, the building was formally opened on 20 October 1973 after a gestation beginning with Utzon's 1957 selection as winner of an international design competition.

Architecture

The Sydney Opera House is a masterpiece of late modern architecture. Its design features a series of large precast concrete "shells", each taken from a sphere of the same radius, forming the roof of the structure, set on a monumental podium. The building covers 4.4 acres and is 183 meters long and about 120 meters wide at its widest point.

Location

The Sydney Opera House is located on Bennelong Point in Sydney Harbour, close to the Sydney Harbour Bridge. It is easily accessible by public transport, including buses, trains, and ferries. The address is Bennelong Point, Sydney NSW 2000, Australia.
