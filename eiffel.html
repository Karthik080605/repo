About the Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.

Constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair, it was initially criticized by some of France's leading artists and intellectuals for its design, but it has become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world.

The Eiffel Tower is the most-visited paid monument in the world; 6.91 million people ascended it in 2015. The tower is 324 meters (1,063 ft) tall, about the same height as an 81-story building, and the tallest structure in Paris.

Distance from India to Paris

The distance from India to Paris, France is approximately 7,349 kilometers (4,567 miles). The most common way to travel between these two locations is by air, with several direct flights available from major Indian cities like New Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore to Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport.

The flight duration from New Delhi to Paris is around 9 hours, while flights from Mumbai and Bangalore take approximately 10 hours. Paris is 4 hours and 30 minutes behind India Standard Time (IST).
