Taj Mahal

The Jewel of Muslim Art in India

History

The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is a mausoleum made of white marble and is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, which combines elements from Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning architecture, featuring a symmetrical layout, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. The main dome rises to 35 meters and is surrounded by four smaller domes. The white marble used in its construction reflects different hues throughout the day, creating a mesmerizing visual experience. The complex also includes a mosque and a guest house.

Location

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the right bank of the Yamuna River. It is easily accessible from major cities in India and is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year.
